WHAT DO YOU DO?

I work with internal and external customers to deliver User-Centered Design services including customer and user research, user experience activities like personas, journeys mapping etc.., and visual design like sketching, mockups, prototypes, etc...

DESCRIBE A TYPICAL WORK DAY FOR YOU.

...

My workday varies depending on the project I am working on. But it is part meetings with stakeholders, part research, part design.

WALK US THROUGH YOUR CAREER JOURNEY. HOW DID YOU GET TO WHERE YOU ARE NOW?

I started off in graphic design in the apparel industry. I felt like I reached a plateau in that industry so I decided to go to school at night for UX Design. I felt like UX Design was a good fit for me because I always want to learn new things and make people's lives easier.

" My advice would be to never stop learning, join UX communities of your choice to network, and approach everything with an open mind."

TIME MACHINE: WHAT ADVICE WOULD YOU GIVE TO YOUR ENTRY-LEVEL SELF?

I would advise myself to reach out to more people in the industry for advice and contacts. I would also advise to be careful who you listen too and never be afraid to be yourself.

WHAT ARE YOU WORKING ON RIGHT NOW?

I am working on the user flow and design for a database dashboard.

WHAT EXCITES YOU THE MOST ABOUT WORKING AS A UX RESEARCHER?

I work with a great team and a company that sees my growth as a designer as an asset.

WHAT ADVICE DO YOU HAVE FOR BEGINNER FOLKS WHO ARE LOOKING TO PLANT ROOTS & BEGIN A CAREER IN YOUR FIELD?

My advice would be to never stop learning, join UX communities of your choice to network, and approach everything with an open mind.
